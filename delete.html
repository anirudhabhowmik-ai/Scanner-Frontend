    Tips From Someone Who Deletes PDF Pages For A Living (Almost)

    üìã Make a list first

    Open your PDF, scroll through, and write down which pages need to go. Doing it from memory guarantees you'll delete the wrong ones. I speak from experience.

    üíæ Keep the original

    Seriously. Save it somewhere with "ORIGINAL" in the filename. You might need it later and won't be able to get it back. I have a folder called "PDF backups" exactly for this.

    üìè Page numbers can be weird

    Some PDFs have Roman numerals for intro pages (i, ii, iii) then start at page 1 later. In our tool, "page 1" means the first page of the file, regardless of what number is printed on it. Keep that in mind.

    üîÑ Combine with other tools

    Delete pages, then compress if it's still huge. Or delete, then merge with other PDFs. Or delete sensitive pages, then share safely. The tools work together.

    ‚úÖ Check your work

    Open the downloaded PDF and make sure everything looks right. Page 15 shouldn't suddenly be next to page 3 if you deleted pages in between. Quick scan saves embarrassment later.

    No. The pages you keep stay exactly the same. Same resolution, same fonts, same everything. Deleting just removes pages - it doesn't re-encode or compress anything.

    Links to websites? Yes. Bookmarks or links that pointed to pages you deleted? Those are broken now. The pages they pointed to don't exist anymore.
