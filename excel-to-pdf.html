    Tips From Someone Who's Converted Hundreds of Spreadsheets

    üìè Check Your Page Setup First

    In Excel, go to Page Layout and see how your sheets will print. If columns are getting cut off, adjust the scaling or change to landscape mode. My cousin learned this after his first report cut off the most important numbers.

    üìã Hide Sheets You Don't Need

    If your workbook has scratch sheets, notes, or raw data you don't want others to see, hide them before converting. The analyst has a "raw data" sheet in every workbook - he hides it before sending to clients.

    üìä Charts Need Space

    Charts that are too close to the edge of the page can get cut off or moved. Give them some breathing room. My cousin's charts used to shift to page 2 until he adjusted their positions.

    üíæ Keep Your Originals

    Always save your original Excel files. The PDF is for sharing, but you'll need the spreadsheet for updates next month. The accountant keeps both - PDF for review, Excel for calculations.

    üî§ Fonts Matter

    Stick to common fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman. Fancy fonts might not display correctly on other systems. The student's professor couldn't read his project once because he used an obscure font - PDF didn't fix that.

    Stuff That Goes Wrong (And How to Fix It)

    üìè "My columns are getting cut off"

    This is the most common problem. In Excel, go to Page Layout and adjust the scaling to "Fit All Columns on One Page" or change to landscape orientation. My cousin's spreadsheets are all landscape now - problem solved.

    üìä "Charts are on different pages than the data"

    Move your charts closer to the relevant data in Excel. If they're too far down, they'll get pushed to the next page. The analyst now places each chart right next to its data table.

    üî¢ "The PDF shows formulas instead of values"

    This shouldn't happen - PDFs should show calculated values. If you're seeing formulas, check if Excel is set to show formulas (Ctrl+` toggles this). Turn that off before converting.

    üìë "Not all sheets are showing up"

    Check if some sheets are hidden in your workbook. Hidden sheets won't convert. Unhide them first, or make sure that's intentional. The HR manager hides raw timesheet data - that's fine.

    üé® "The colors look wrong"

    PDFs preserve colors, but some very light colors might be hard to see. Stick to readable contrast - dark text on light backgrounds. The student's professor couldn't read light gray text on white.
