    What is PDF Page Extraction?

    PDF page extraction is the process of selecting specific pages from a PDF document and creating a new PDF containing only those pages. The original PDF remains completely unchanged. This is different from splitting, which divides a PDF into multiple files, or deleting, which removes pages from the original. Extraction creates a copy of selected pages in a new document.

    Page Number Format Examples

    • Single page: "5" - Extracts only page 5
    • Multiple pages: "2,4,7" - Extracts pages 2, 4, and 7
    • Page range: "10-15" - Extracts pages 10 through 15
    • Combination: "1,3,5-8,12" - Extracts pages 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 12
    • From start: "1-5" - Extracts the first 5 pages
    • From end: "25-30" - Extracts the last 6 pages of a 30-page document

    Extract vs. Split vs. Delete: What's the Difference?

    • Extract Pages: Creates a new PDF containing selected pages from the original. Original remains unchanged.
    • Split PDF: Divides a PDF into multiple separate files (e.g., every 5 pages becomes a new file).
    • Delete Pages: Removes pages from the original PDF and saves the remaining pages as a new file.
    • Our Tool: Focuses on extraction — perfect when you need a copy of specific pages.
