    How to Choose the Right Settings

    üìç Position: Where do numbers go?

    Bottom center - Standard for most documents. Looks clean, doesn't distract.
    Top right - Common in academic papers and manuscripts.
    Bottom right - Traditional for books and formal reports.
    Bottom left - Less common, but works if your document is bound on the right side.

    üî§ Font: It matters more than you think

    Helvetica (or Arial) - Clean, modern, works for most business documents.
    Times Roman - Traditional, academic, looks formal.
    Courier - Typewriter style, technical documents, coding manuals.
    Just pick something that matches your document's body text. Mixing fonts looks messy.

    üìè Size: Big enough to see, small enough to ignore

    10-12pt is standard for most documents. If your body text is 11pt, make page numbers 10-11pt. Too big and they look clunky. Too small and no one sees them.

    üìù Format: Just numbers or something fancier?

    Simple: "5" - Clean, minimal, works for everything.
    Page X of Y: "Page 5 of 42" - Helps readers know how much is left. Great for reports.
    With dashes: "- 5 -" - Traditional book style.
    Custom: "Section 2 - Page 5" - Use the custom text box with {n} for page number and {total} for total pages.

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Stuff I learned the hard way: If you have a title page or cover page, set the start page to 2. Nobody numbers the cover.

    Stuff That Goes Wrong (And How to Fix It)

    üìç "The numbers are in the wrong spot"

    That's why the preview exists. Look at it before you hit download. If they're already downloaded, just do it again with the right position. The original file is still there.

    üî¢ "The page numbers don't match my printed numbers"

    Some PDFs have Roman numerals in the front (i, ii, iii) then start at 1 later. Our tool counts actual PDF pages, not printed numbers. Page 1 is the first page of the file, even if it says "i" on it.

    üìè "Numbers overlap with my content"

    Try a different position. If bottom center overlaps a footer, switch to bottom right. Or make the font smaller. Or adjust your PDF's margins before numbering.

    üîí "It won't let me add numbers"

    Password protected? Can't edit locked files. Use the Unlock PDF tool first, then come back here.

    üêå "The preview is loading slowly"

    Big PDFs take time to generate previews. A 200-page document might need a minute. If it's genuinely stuck, try a different browser or refresh.

    üìù "My custom text isn't working"

    Use {n} for page number and {total} for total pages. Example: "Page {n} of {total}" becomes "Page 5 of 42". Make sure you're using curly brackets, not regular parentheses.
