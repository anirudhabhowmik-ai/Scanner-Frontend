    Extraction Methods Explained

    • Auto (Recommended): Automatically detects the best extraction method based on your PDF's structure. Works for most documents.
    • Tabula: Best for simple, well-structured tables with clear column boundaries. Fast and accurate for basic tables.
    • Camelot: Advanced extraction for complex tables, nested headers, and irregular layouts. Handles merged cells well.
    • Lattice Mode: Optimized for tables with visible borders and grid lines. Perfect for bordered tables.
    • Stream Mode: Designed for borderless tables where whitespace defines columns. Works well for text-based tables.

    Tips for Best Results

    • High Quality Source: Higher resolution scans produce better extraction results.
    • Clear Table Structure: Tables with consistent columns and clear borders extract most accurately.
    • Try Different Methods: If Auto doesn't work perfectly, try Lattice (for bordered tables) or Stream (for borderless tables).
    • Extract Specific Pages: If your PDF has many pages, extract only the pages with tables to save time.
    • Check Output Format: Use .xlsx for maximum compatibility with modern Excel versions.

    PDF to Excel vs. PDF to CSV: Which to Choose?

    • Excel (.xlsx/.xls): Best for further data analysis, formulas, charts, and formatting. Preserves multiple sheets.
    • CSV (Comma Separated Values): Best for importing into databases, data processing tools, or when you need a lightweight format. No formatting or multiple sheets.
    • Our Recommendation: Use Excel format for most business and analysis needs. Use CSV when you need to import data into specialized software.
