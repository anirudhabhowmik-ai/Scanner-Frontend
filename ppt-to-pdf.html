    Tips From Someone Who's Converted Hundreds of Presentations

    üî§ Stick to Safe Fonts

    Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman - these work everywhere. Fancy fonts you downloaded from some website? They might not embed properly. My pitch deck used a cool font that my partner didn't have - whole thing looked wrong. Now I stick to basics.

    üì∏ Check Your Images

    Low-res images in PowerPoint become low-res images in PDF. If you need crisp photos, make sure they're high quality before you insert them. The sales director learned this when a client couldn't read text in a screenshot.

    üé¨ Animations Won't Work

    PDF is static. If you have slides with animations, the PDF will show the final state of the slide. No clicks, no reveals, no transitions. If you need animations, stick to PowerPoint. If you need everyone to see the same thing, use PDF.

    Stuff That Goes Wrong (And How to Fix It)

    üî§ "The fonts look different in the PDF"

    This usually means you used a font that isn't standard. Switch to Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman, then convert again. My pitch deck disaster was exactly this - fancy font, replaced with Arial, problem solved.

    üì∏ "My images are blurry"

    Check your original images. If they're low-res in PowerPoint, they'll be low-res in PDF. Replace them with higher quality versions before converting. The sales director learned this lesson the hard way.

    üé¨ "My animations are missing"

    PDF doesn't support animations. Each slide becomes a static page showing the final state. If you need animations, stick to PowerPoint. If you need everyone to see the same thing, PDF is the way to go.

    üìè "The slide size is wrong"

    PDF matches your PowerPoint slide size. If you want different dimensions, change the slide size in PowerPoint first. Standard 4:3 or 16:9 both work fine.

    üìã "Speaker notes are missing"

    Speaker notes aren't included in the PDF. Only the slides themselves convert. If you need notes in your PDF, add them to the slide content before converting.
