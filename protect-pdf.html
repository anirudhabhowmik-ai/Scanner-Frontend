    What PDF Password Protection Does

    • User Password: Prevents unauthorized people from opening or viewing the PDF. Recipients must enter the correct password to access the document.
    • Permissions Password: Restricts what users can do with the document (printing, copying, editing) while still allowing them to view it.
    • 128-bit AES Encryption: Industry-standard encryption that ensures your documents are secure from brute-force attacks.
    • Prevent Printing: Stop recipients from printing your document, protecting physical copies from being distributed.
    • Prevent Copying: Disable copying of text and images, preventing content from being extracted and reused.
    • Prevent Editing: Block modifications to the document, including annotations, form filling, and page extraction.

    Password Best Practices

    • Use Strong Passwords: At least 8 characters, mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
    • Avoid Common Words: Don't use "password", "123456", "admin", or personal information like birthdays.
    • Use Unique Passwords: Use a different password for each protected document.
    • Share Separately: Never send the password in the same email or message as the protected PDF.
    • Consider Password Managers: Use a password manager to generate and store strong passwords.
    • Update Regularly: Change passwords periodically for highly sensitive documents.

    Understanding Encryption Levels

    • No Encryption: Anyone can open, view, print, copy, and edit the PDF without restrictions.
    • Password Protection (Our Tool): 128-bit AES encryption. Recipients need the password to open the document.
    • With Permissions: Same encryption level, plus restrictions on printing, copying, and editing.
    • 256-bit AES: Higher encryption level for maximum security (not available in all tools).

    When to Use Password Protection

    • Storing in Cloud: Add an extra layer of security to documents stored in cloud services.
    • Sharing with External Parties: Protect client deliverables and third-party communications.
    • Legal Compliance: GDPR, HIPAA, and other regulations may require document encryption.
    • Preventing Unauthorized Distribution: Disable printing and copying to control document distribution.
