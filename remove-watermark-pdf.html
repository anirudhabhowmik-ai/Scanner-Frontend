    Types of Watermarks You Can Remove

    • Text Watermarks: Words like "Confidential", "Draft", "Sample", "Do Not Copy", company names, or any text overlaying your document.
    • Image Watermarks: Logos, stamps, signatures, or any graphic elements placed as watermarks on your PDF pages.
    • Vector Graphics: Shapes, lines, borders, or decorative elements that act as watermarks.
    • Multiple Watermarks: Remove several watermarks from the same page or across multiple pages.

    Common Use Cases for Removing PDF Watermarks

    • Clean Client Deliverables: Remove "Draft" or "Sample" watermarks before sending final documents to clients.
    • Professional Documents: Clean up resumes, proposals, and reports that have unwanted watermark overlays.
    • Academic Papers: Remove institution watermarks or "Review Copy" text from research papers and theses.
    • Legal Documents: Clean contracts, agreements, and legal correspondence for professional presentation.
    • Business Reports: Remove internal watermarking before sharing reports with external stakeholders.
    • Scanned Documents: Eliminate scanner-generated watermarks or timestamps from scanned PDFs.
    • Archived Files: Clean up archived documents that have outdated or unnecessary watermark overlays.
