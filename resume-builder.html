      Why Your Resume Needs to Be ATS-Friendly

      • Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS): Most large companies use ATS software to filter resumes before a human sees them.
      • Keyword Matching: ATS scans for keywords from the job description — missing keywords means automatic rejection.
      • Simple Formatting: Complex tables, columns, graphics, and unusual fonts can confuse ATS software.
      • Our Tool: Our single-column and sidebar layouts are ATS-optimized. Plain text export is also available for maximum compatibility.

      Resume Writing Tips from Our AI

      • Use Action Verbs: Start bullet points with words like "Led", "Developed", "Increased", "Managed", "Created".
      • Quantify Achievements: Use numbers, percentages, and dollar amounts (e.g., "Increased sales by 25%").
      • Tailor to Each Job: Customize your summary and skills for every application.
      • Keep It Concise: One page for early-career, two pages for experienced professionals.
      • Proofread: Use our preview feature to check for errors before exporting.

      Export Formats Explained

      • PDF (Recommended): Print-ready, preserves all formatting. Best for email applications and online job portals.
      • Word (.docx): Editable in Microsoft Word and Google Docs. Great for further customization.
      • HTML: Self-contained web file — open in any browser.
      • Plain Text (.txt): ATS-optimized format with no formatting. Paste directly into online application forms.
      • JSON Backup: Save your resume data to restore later or edit programmatically.
