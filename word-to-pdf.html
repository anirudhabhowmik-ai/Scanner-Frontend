    Tips From Someone Who's Converted Hundreds of Word Docs

    üî§ Stick to Common Fonts

    Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman - these are safe. Fancy fonts that you downloaded from somewhere might not embed properly. My resume used a custom font once - the recruiter saw something completely different. Now I stick to basics.

    üì∏ Check Your Images

    Low-res images in Word become low-res images in PDF. If you need crisp photos or graphics, make sure they're high quality before you insert them. The freelancer's proposals include portfolio images - she checks every one before converting.

    üìã Turn Off Track Changes

    If you have edits showing in your Word doc, they'll show up in the PDF. Accept or reject all changes before converting. The paralegal once sent a contract with "INSERT CLAUSE HERE" still visible - embarrassing.

    üíæ Keep the Word Original

    Always save your original Word file. PDFs are for sharing, but you'll need the Word version for editing next month. The HR manager keeps both - Word for updates, PDF for sending.

    üîó Hyperlinks Still Work

    Got links in your document? They stay clickable in the PDF. The freelancer includes links to her online portfolio - clients can click right through from the PDF.

    Stuff That Goes Wrong (And How to Fix It)

    üî§ "The fonts look different in the PDF"

    This usually means you used a font that isn't standard. Switch to Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman, then convert again. My resume problem was exactly this - fancy font, replaced with Arial, problem solved.

    üì∏ "My images are blurry"

    Check your original images. If they're low-res in Word, they'll be low-res in PDF. Replace them with higher quality versions before converting. The freelancer learned this when a client couldn't read text in a screenshot.

    üìÑ "The page breaks are wrong"

    PDF follows the page layout you set in Word. Go to Page Layout and check your margins, orientation, and manual page breaks. Adjust in Word, then convert again.

    üìã "Track changes are showing up"

    Turn off Track Changes in Word and accept or reject all edits. Then save and convert. The paralegal's "INSERT CLAUSE HERE" mistake taught her this lesson.

    üîó "My links don't work in the PDF"

    Check that your links are properly formatted in Word - blue and underlined usually means they're recognized as hyperlinks. If they're plain text, they won't be clickable.
